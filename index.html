Adoption Personas

Click on a persona to see details, triggers, and intervention needs

Policy-paralyzed

Believes AI is risky or forbidden. Hesitates even when use would be permissible.

Trigger
Confusing rules, client data concerns, unclear "allowed vs. not" boundaries
Needs
Clear decision trees, scenario-based examples, quick compliance checks
Permission Stage
Access-limited

Wants to use AI but can't get to the right tools. Stuck with limited internal options.

Trigger
Missing licenses, restricted permissions, no process to request access
Needs
Access provisioning, clear request process, tool alternatives
Access Stage
Tool-disappointed

Tried AI once and it underperformed. Wrote it off based on early bad experience.

Trigger
Poor outputs from internal chatbot at launch, no guidance on better approaches
Needs
Re-onboarding, "what's changed" messaging, second-chance demos
Capability Stage
Low-confidence

Unsure how to get good outputs. Training didn't translate to real work.

Trigger
Generic training, no prompt guidance, doesn't know when to trust outputs
Needs
Role-specific workshops, prompt libraries, QA checklists, hands-on practice
Capability Stage
ROI-skeptic

Doesn't believe AI adds real value for their specific work. Sees it as hype.

Trigger
No concrete examples in their domain, established efficient workflows
Needs
Value demonstration, time-savings data, before/after case studies, quick wins
Fit Stage
Time-starved

Would try AI but has no slack time. Billable hour pressure crowds out learning.

Trigger
High utilization targets, no protected learning time, always on deadline
Needs
Protected experimentation time, micro-learning modules, embedded-in-workflow tools
Fit Stage
Workflow-stranded

Has skills and access, but available tools don't fit their actual work.

Trigger
"I tried it but it doesn't help with proposals / decks / my real tasks"
Needs
Tool-task fit guides, workflow-specific solutions, embedded AI in existing tools
Fit Stage
Incentive-anxious

Worried about job security or being given more work if they become more efficient.

Trigger
"Will this replace me?" or "Will I just get piled with more work?"
Needs
Role evolution narrative, explicit benefit-sharing, leadership commitment on workload
Incentives Stage
Unconvinced observer

Waiting to see others succeed first. Has access and skills but needs social proof.

Trigger
"Show me someone on my type of project who's actually done this successfully"
Needs
Peer case studies, champion network, visible success stories from respected colleagues
